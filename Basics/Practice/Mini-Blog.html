Champaran and Kheda Satyagraha
THE Champaran Agitation and Kheda Satyagraha of 1918 was the frist major success of Mahatma Gandhi in his stuggle towards India's freedom. The reason for the agitation was the levy of an oppressive tax by the British, which they insisted on increasing further. He organized his supporters as well as volunteers to protest against this atrocity and also began leading the clean up of villages,building of schools and hospitals as well as encouraging the village leadership to condemn the numerous social evils affecting the society. Mahatma Gandhi was successful in singning an agreement with the Britrish, where the poor farmers were granted more compensation and control over farming.
